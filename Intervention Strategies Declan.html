üß† 2. Key Intervention Strategies

A. Identifying the Critical Voice (Internal Dialogue)

The first step is to "catch" that automatic voice. We're going to shine a spotlight on it to create cognitive distance.

1. Automatic Thought Register

For several days, write down the automatic phrases that appear when you miss a task or skip a training session. For example:

  • "I am lazy."
  • "I am wasting time."
  • "I don't deserve to rest."

2. The Teammate's Perspective

Then, in another column, write what you would say to a respected teammate if they were in the *exact* same situation.

3. Compare and Reflect

Compare the two columns and reflect:

  • What tone do you use with yourself? Is it hostile, supportive, or neutral?
  • Would you say those same words to someone you respect? Why not?

üß† Effect: Cognitive Distance

This exposes the harshness of your internal dialogue. It allows you to see the thought instead of seeing *from* the thought.

C. Psychoeducation: Rest is Productivity

**Key Concept:** Rest is not a reward for performing; it is a fundamental part of your training (**Active Recovery**).

üîã

The Engine Metaphor and the Energy Curve

If an engine works non-stop, it burns out. Your performance is a curve: if it doesn't drop (rest), it's impossible for it to rise (supercompensation). **Rest is the scheduled rise.**

D. Practice: Acceptance and Self-Compassion

The goal is not to eliminate self-criticism, but to change your response to it.

3-Step Mini Ritual Against Guilt

  1. **Acknowledge and Name:** "I am feeling guilt/insufficiency. This is tough."
  2. **Normalize:** "Many athletes feel this. It is a normal reaction to pressure."
  3. **Respond with a Tool:** "I acknowledge that I feel guilty, but rest is not a failure, **it is a high-performance tool**."

üèÅ 3. Transfer to the World Championship

Managing Pre-Competition Fatigue

**Golden Rule:** Do not interpret fatigue and the tapering phase as weakness or lack of commitment. It is the sign that the hard work is done and your body is preparing for the final explosion. Distinguish between:

  • **Necessary Rest:** The rest that brings you closer to peak form.
  • **Avoidance:** The rest you use to avoid facing a task.
